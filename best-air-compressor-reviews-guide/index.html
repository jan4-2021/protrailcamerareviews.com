TYPES OF AIR COMPRESSORS

Single Stage or Dual Stage

You can usually find reciprocating (aka Piston) Compressors in two styles; Single Stage and Two Stage. A single stage compressor works by taking air in and compressing the air to its final pressure in one piston stroke.

Single stage air compressors can achieve max pressures of up to 150 PSI. Usually, a single stage air compressorÂ can achieve higher CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute) specifications than a two stage airÂ compressor, because every cylinder stroke is bringingÂ in air and compressing it with air withÂ every rotation.

Dual stage air compressors functionÂ in a similar way. The mainÂ difference is they compress air in 2 separate stages.

  • First stage: air enters a chamberÂ and compressed to a medium pressure.
  • Second stage: the air that wasÂ compressed in the first stage is pumped through an intercooler where the air is cooled and compressed in the final or second stage. Dual stage air compressors are usually capable of pressures up to 200 psi. Dual stage compressors are the most efficient at higher pressures because of the air cooling stage in between.

Maintenance

There are two kinds of air compressors, oil and oil-free:

  • Deciding whether or not you want to maintain you air compressor comes down to choosing between two types oil and oil free. The main difference between an oil and an oil free motors are that an oil based compressor, just like a combustible engine, requires regular maintenance to make sure the oil is clean and at the appropriate levels to ensure proper lubrication, help keep the compressor cool, and prevent moving parts from seizing.Â Generally speaking you will find oil based compressors in more powerful, commercial models.
  • Oil-free compressors do still use oil, but they are designed with sealed bearings. In other words, you never have to deal with checkingÂ oil levels. Oiled pumps tend to run cooler and last longer than oil-free pumps.
